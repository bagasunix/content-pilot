# Content Measurement Framework (Research-Based)

## Research Basis
- **Backlinko 2026**: "Organize metrics into two categories: business impact and engagement"
- **HubSpot**: content ROI requires connecting traffic to revenue
- **Industry**: most blogs track vanity metrics, not business metrics

## Two-Category Framework

### Category 1: Business Impact Metrics
**Directly connect to revenue and growth.**

| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Conversions | Actions taken (signup, purchase) | > 2% of traffic |
| Revenue | Direct revenue from content | Growing MoM |
| Leads Generated | Qualified leads from content | > 5 per article |
| Email Signups | Newsletter subscriptions | > 1% of visitors |
| Affiliate Clicks | Monetization performance | Growing MoM |
| Cost Per Acquisition | Cost to acquire customer via content | Decreasing |

### Category 2: Engagement Metrics
**Show how well content resonates.**

| Metric | What It Measures | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| Organic Traffic | Search-driven visitors | Growing 15-20% MoM |
| Engagement Rate | Active sessions / total sessions | > 60% |
| Avg Engagement Time | Time spent on page | > 2 minutes |
| Pages Per Session | Depth of engagement | > 2 pages |
| Return Visitor Rate | Repeat visitors | > 30% |
| Social Shares | Content distribution | Growing |
| Comments | Community engagement | > 5 per post |

## ROI Calculation

### Content ROI Formula
```
Content ROI = (Revenue from Content - Cost of Content) / Cost of Content × 100

Where:
Revenue = Direct revenue (affiliate, ads) + Attribution revenue (leads → sales)
Cost = Writer time + Research time + Promotion time + Tools
```

### Per-Article ROI
```
Article ROI = (Traffic × RPM) - (Writer Cost + Promotion Cost)

Example:
Traffic: 5,000 monthly visits
RPM: $5 (AdSense)
Revenue: $25/month
Writer cost: $50 (one-time)
Promotion cost: $10 (one-time)
ROI: ($25 × 12) - $60 = $240/year
ROI %: ($240 - $60) / $60 × 100 = 300%
```

### Content Type ROI Comparison
| Content Type | Cost | Traffic | Revenue | ROI |
|-------------|------|---------|---------|-----|
| How-to Guide | $50 | 5K/mo | $25/mo | 300%/yr |
| Product Review | $50 | 3K/mo | $50/mo (affiliate) | 900%/yr |
| News Post | $20 | 1K/mo | $5/mo | 200%/yr |
| Original Research | $200 | 10K/mo | $50/mo | 200%/yr |

## Awareness-Stage Conversion Tracking

### Per-Stage Metrics
| Stage | Primary Metric | Conversion Target | Tracking Method |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Most Aware | Purchase/signup | > 5% | GA4 conversion |
| Product Aware | Trial/demo | > 3% | GA4 conversion |
| Solution Aware | Newsletter signup | > 2% | Email platform |
| Problem Aware | Return visit | > 30% | GA4 cohort |
| Unaware | Social follow | > 1% | Social analytics |

### Stage-to-Revenue Attribution
```
Most Aware content → Direct revenue (track via UTM)
Product Aware content → Trial → Conversion (track in CRM)
Solution Aware content → Newsletter → Nurture → Sale (track in email)
Problem Aware content → Return visits → Eventually convert (long-term)
Unaware content → Brand awareness → Indirect (harder to track)
```

## Decision Framework

### What to Create More Of
- Content with highest ROI
- Content driving most conversions
- Content in under-served awareness stages
- Content with highest engagement

### What to Optimize
- High traffic + low conversion (fix CTA)
- High conversion + low traffic (promote more)
- Low traffic + high engagement (expand reach)
- High traffic + low engagement (improve quality)

### What to Stop Creating
- Consistently low ROI content
- Content that doesn't serve any awareness stage
- Content with zero engagement
- Content that cannibalizes higher-performing content

## Pitfalls
1. Don't track only vanity metrics (pageviews, impressions)
2. Don't ignore attribution — connect traffic to revenue
3. Don't skip awareness-stage tracking
4. Don't forget to compare MoM and QoQ trends
5. Don't make decisions without data
