# Awareness Stage Strategy (Research-Based)

## Research Basis
- **Backlinko**: "Start with people ready to buy (most aware) and work backward — faster wins, better conversion, less competition"
- **Eugene Schwartz**: 5 awareness levels (Breakthrough Advertising, 1966)
- **Industry**: content mapped to awareness stage converts 3-5x better than generic

## The 5 Awareness Stages

### Stage 1: Most Aware
**Who**: Ready to buy. Know your product. Just need final push.
**Content Type**:
- Customer success stories / case studies
- Pricing guides and ROI calculators
- Free trial offers with time limits
- Comparison pages (you vs competitor)
- Demo request pages

**CTA**: Buy, sign up, request demo, start trial
**Metrics**: Demo requests, pricing page visits, free trials
**Example**: "Semrush Success Stories page — each story ends with free trial CTA"

### Stage 2: Product Aware
**Who**: Know they need a solution. Comparing options. Reading reviews.
**Content Type**:
- Product walkthrough videos
- Comparison posts ("X vs Y")
- Review roundups ("Best SEO tools 2026")
- Feature comparison tables
- Influencer/customer reviews

**CTA**: Start free trial, compare features, read reviews
**Metrics**: Comparison page visits, feature page engagement
**Example**: "Backlinko's 'Best SEO Tools' — honest comparison with clear differentiators"

### Stage 3: Solution Aware
**Who**: Know solutions exist. Figuring out what kind they need.
**Content Type**:
- How-to guides and educational content
- "What is [solution type]" articles
- Podcast episodes / interviews
- Interactive tools / quizzes
- Video testimonials

**CTA**: Download guide, subscribe to newsletter, watch tutorial
**Metrics**: Guide downloads, newsletter signups
**Example**: "Ahrefs 'How to do keyword research' — educates while positioning tool"

### Stage 4: Problem Aware
**Who**: Know they have a problem. Don't know what to do about it.
**Content Type**:
- Blog posts exploring root causes
- Social content highlighting symptoms
- YouTube videos breaking down pain points
- Forum posts sharing perspectives
- "Why is this happening" content

**CTA**: Read more, follow on social, join community
**Metrics**: Blog subscriptions, social follows
**Example**: "Why is my blog not getting traffic? — validates problem, hints at solutions"

### Stage 5: Unaware
**Who**: Don't know they have a problem yet. Biggest and coldest audience.
**Content Type**:
- Light, relatable content for discovery
- Social media content (short-form)
- Entertainment/educational hybrid
- Trend commentary
- Industry insights

**CTA**: Follow, share, engage
**Metrics**: Reach, impressions, shares
**Example**: "5 signs your website is losing money you don't know about"

## Mapping Process

### Step 1: Assign Stage to Topic
```
For each topic in content calendar:
1. What does the reader KNOW when they search this?
   → Most Aware: searching for your product name
   → Product Aware: searching "best [category]"
   → Solution Aware: searching "how to [solve problem]"
   → Problem Aware: searching "why [problem exists]"
   → Unaware: not searching (social/discovery only)

2. What's their INTENT?
   → Ready to buy = Most/Product Aware
   → Evaluating options = Solution Aware
   → Understanding problem = Problem/Unaware
```

### Step 2: Choose Content Format
| Stage | Format | Length | Depth |
|-------|--------|--------|-------|
| Most Aware | Case study, comparison, pricing | Short | High conversion |
| Product Aware | Review, walkthrough, comparison | Medium | Feature-focused |
| Solution Aware | How-to, guide, tutorial | Long | Educational |
| Problem Aware | Blog post, video, social | Medium | Empathy + education |
| Unaware | Social, video, infographic | Short | Discovery + engagement |

### Step 3: Set CTA per Stage
| Stage | CTA Type | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Most Aware | Direct conversion | "Start free trial" |
| Product Aware | Consideration | "Compare features" |
| Solution Aware | Lead capture | "Download guide" |
| Problem Aware | Engagement | "Read more" |
| Unaware | Discovery | "Follow us" |

### Step 4: Define Success Metrics
| Stage | Primary Metric | Target |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Most Aware | Conversion rate | > 5% |
| Product Aware | Trial signups | > 3% |
| Solution Aware | Newsletter signups | > 2% |
| Problem Aware | Return visits | > 30% |
| Unaware | Social shares | > 100 |

## Strategy Framework

### Work Backward (Backlinko Method)
1. Start with Most Aware content (fastest ROI)
2. Build Product Aware content (comparison pages)
3. Create Solution Aware content (educational)
4. Add Problem Aware content (awareness)
5. Layer Unaware content (discovery)

### Content Mix Recommendation
| Stage | % of Content | Why |
|-------|-------------|-----|
| Most Aware | 15% | High conversion, low volume |
| Product Aware | 20% | Comparison traffic, decision stage |
| Solution Aware | 30% | Highest search volume, educational |
| Problem Aware | 25% | Problem validation, social sharing |
| Unaware | 10% | Discovery, brand awareness |

## Pitfalls
1. Don't create all content for one stage — balance the funnel
2. Don't put hard sell on Problem Aware content — builds distrust
3. Don't skip Most Aware — fastest path to revenue
4. Don't assume awareness stage — validate with search intent
5. Don't forget to track per-stage metrics

## Verification
- All topics assigned to awareness stages
- Content format matches stage
- CTA appropriate for stage
- Metrics defined per stage
