# E-E-A-T Framework (Research-Based)

## Research Basis
- **Google Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines**: E-E-A-T is core ranking signal
- **Backlinko**: "Bring in expert perspectives — hire a plumber to write about plumbing"
- **Ahrefs**: E-E-A-T signals correlate with top rankings
- **Industry**: AI content without E-E-A-T = thin content penalty risk

## What Is E-E-A-T

### 4 Pillars
1. **Experience**: First-hand experience with the topic
2. **Expertise**: Deep knowledge and skill
3. **Authoritativeness**: Recognized reputation in the field
4. **Trustworthiness**: Transparent, accurate, reliable

### Why It Matters (2026)
- **Google ranking signal**: especially for YMYL (Your Money Your Life) topics
- **AI search**: AI cites authoritative, trustworthy sources
- **Reader trust**: E-E-A-T signals build credibility
- **Content differentiation**: human experience = competitive moat vs AI

## Experience

### How to Demonstrate
- **Personal anecdotes**: "When I started my blog in 2014..."
- **Screenshots**: real dashboard, real results
- **Case studies**: specific examples with data
- **Before/after**: show transformation
- **Process documentation**: "Here's exactly how I did it"

### Content Signals
```
✓ "I tested this tool for 3 months"
✓ "Here's my actual dashboard showing..."
✓ "After 200 blog posts, I learned..."
✓ "This is the exact process I use"
✗ Generic advice without personal experience
✗ "Many experts say..." without specifics
✗ Theoretical content without practical examples
```

### For Blog {{DOMAIN}}
- Show real Blogger analytics
- Document actual SEO experiments
- Share real traffic numbers
- Include personal screenshots
- Tell stories from 10+ years of blogging

## Expertise

### How to Demonstrate
- **Depth of coverage**: comprehensive, detailed content
- **Technical accuracy**: correct terminology, concepts
- **Nuanced analysis**: not surface-level advice
- **Source citation**: reference authoritative sources
- **Professional credentials**: author bio highlights

### Content Signals
```
✓ Detailed technical explanations
✓ Correct use of industry terminology
✓ Nuanced analysis (not just pros/cons)
✓ References to authoritative sources
✓ Author bio with relevant experience
✗ Shallow coverage of complex topics
✗ Incorrect technical information
✗ No sources cited
```

## Authoritativeness

### How to Build
- **Consistent publishing**: regular, high-quality content
- **External recognition**: guest posts, interviews, features
- **Backlinks**: other sites linking to your content
- **Social proof**: follower count, engagement, shares
- **Industry contributions**: speaking, writing, teaching

### Author Bio Optimization
```
[Name] — [Title/Role]
[Relevant experience: years, topics, achievements]
[Credentials: certifications, publications, awards]
[Contact: email, social links]
```

### For Blog
- Consistent author identity across all posts
- Author bio on every article
- Links to social profiles
- Mention of relevant experience
- External contributions (guest posts, forums)

## Trustworthiness

### How to Demonstrate
- **Accuracy**: fact-check all claims
- **Transparency**: disclose affiliations, biases
- **Citations**: link to primary sources
- **Corrections**: update errors promptly
- **Privacy**: clear privacy policy, data handling

### Content Signals
```
✓ All statistics cited with sources
✓ Affiliate links disclosed
✓ Personal experience clearly stated
✓ Limitations acknowledged
✓ Privacy policy visible
✗ Unsubstantiated claims
✗ Hidden affiliate links
✗ Outdated information not updated
✗ No source attribution
```

### Trust Signals Checklist
- [ ] HTTPS enabled
- [ ] Privacy policy visible
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure on relevant pages
- [ ] Author bio with credentials
- [ ] Contact information available
- [ ] About page with team info
- [ ] Regular content updates

## Author Bio Template

### Standard Bio
```
**[Name]** is [role] at [company] with [X] years of experience in [topic].
[He/She] has [specific achievement: published in, worked with, etc.].
Connect: [Twitter] [LinkedIn] [Email]
```

### Expert Bio
```
**[Name]** is a [certification] professional specializing in [topic].
With [X] years of experience, [he/she] has [specific achievements].
[His/Her] work has been featured in [publications].
Connect: [Twitter] [LinkedIn] [Email]
```

## Pitfalls
1. Don't claim expertise without experience — readers detect fakes
2. Don't skip citations — uncredited claims hurt trust
3. Don't ignore author bio — it's your credibility anchor
4. Don't forget to update outdated content — stale = untrustworthy
5. Don't hide affiliate relationships — transparency builds trust

## Verification
- Every article has author bio
- All claims cited with sources
- Affiliate links disclosed
- Screenshots show real data
- Content demonstrates first-hand experience
- Regular content updates documented
