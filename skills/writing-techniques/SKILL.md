# Writing Techniques — Quick Reference

## The 17 Techniques

### Making Ideas Clear
1. **Metaphor** — Compare A to B without "like/as". Best when point feels abstract.
2. **Simile** — Compare using "like" or "as". Safer than metaphor, quick image.
3. **Analogy** — Break complex idea using something familiar. Teaching without sounding like a teacher.

### Making Writing Vivid
4. **Imagery** — Help readers SEE what you describe. Use when writing feels bland/generic.
5. **Personification** — Give human traits to non-human things. Light touch = alive. Too much = forced.
6. **Sensory words** — Sight, sound, taste, touch, smell. Difference between readable and vivid.
7. **Strong verbs** — Replace weak "make/do/go" with vivid verbs. Quick fix for flat writing.

### Making Writing Stick
8. **Alliteration** — Repeat starting sounds. Makes phrases memorable. Light touch.
9. **Repetition** — Reinforce by saying more than once. Use to drive home an idea.
10. **Rule of three** — Group ideas in threes. Feels balanced and complete. WARNING: overused by AI.

### Making Writing Flow
11. **Parallelism** — Similar sentence structures create balance. Writing feels smoother.
12. **Sentence structure** — Vary length and pattern. Prevents monotony.
13. **Contrast** — Put opposing ideas side by side. Creates tension and clarity.

### Making Writing Land
14. **Clear writing** — Remove anything slowing reader down. Clarity > cleverness.
15. **Eliminating filler words** — Cut words adding length without meaning.
16. **Point of view** — Who's telling the story? Shifts tone completely.
17. **Foreshadowing** — Hint at what's coming. Builds anticipation.

## Diagnostic Guide — What to Fix

| Problem | Technique to Use |
|---------|-----------------|
| Writing feels FLAT | Imagery, sensory words, metaphors/similes |
| Sentence works but feels WEAK | Strong verbs |
| Readers start DRIFTING | Sentence structure, parallelism, alliteration |
| Point makes sense but doesn't LAND | Clear writing, eliminating fillers, analogies |
| Something feels DULL | Contrast, personification |
| Want a line to LAND | Repetition, rule of three (light touch) |
| Want control over overall FEEL | Point of view, foreshadowing |
| Nothing stands out | Pick ONE thing off, fix that first. Don't stack. |

## Anti-AI Rules
- Avoid overusing rule of three (AI overuses it)
- Don't stack multiple techniques in one sentence
- Prefer clarity over cleverness
- Use sensory words for vividness, not decoration
