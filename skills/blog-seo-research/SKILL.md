# Blog SEO Research (Google + Ahrefs Framework)

## Research Sources
- **Google SEO Starter Guide** (official)
- **Ahrefs Keyword Research Guide** (Tim Soulo, CMO)

## Google's Official Best Practices

### Content Quality (Google's Words)
- Content must be **helpful, reliable, and people-first**
- **Easy-to-read, well-organized, unique, up-to-date**
- Don't copy others — create based on own knowledge
- Check previously published content and update as needed

### Site Organization
- **Descriptive URLs** with words users understand
- **Group topically similar pages** in directories
- **Reduce duplicate content** — use canonical URLs
- Organize logically for crawlers and users

### Title & Snippet Optimization
- Title: **unique, clear, concise**, accurately describes content
- Include website name and relevant info
- Meta description controls snippet
- Snippet sourced from actual page content

### Image SEO
- **High-quality images** near relevant text
- **Descriptive alt text** explaining image-page relationship
- Images help visual search discovery

### Linking Best Practices
- **Descriptive anchor text** for all links
- Links demonstrate topic expertise
- Trust resources you link to
- UGC links need nofollow

## Ahrefs Keyword Research Framework

### Step 1: Seed Keywords
**From customer perspective**: What do they search to find your content?
**From competitor perspective**: What do competitors rank for?
**From existing data**: What does your site already rank for?

### Step 2: Keyword Metrics (Priority Order)
1. **Business Potential** (1-3): How valuable for your blog?
2. **Traffic Potential**: Total organic traffic if ranking #1
3. **Keyword Difficulty** (0-100): How hard to rank?
4. **Search Volume**: Monthly searches
5. **CPC**: Commercial intent indicator

### Step 3: Search Intent Classification
- **Informational**: "how to", "what is", "cara", "apa itu"
- **Navigational**: brand/product searches
- **Transactional**: "buy", "price", "beli", "harga"
- **Commercial Investigation**: "best", "review", "terbaik"

### Step 4: Keyword Prioritization Matrix
| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| High volume + Low KD + High business | **Top priority** |
| High volume + High KD | **Long-term investment** |
| Low volume + High business | **Worth creating** |
| High KD + Low business | **Skip** |

### Key Ahrefs Data
- **90% of pages get no organic traffic** (study of 1B pages)
- Traffic Potential > Search Volume (better metric)
- Keyword clusters > individual keywords
- Matching terms + Related terms = comprehensive coverage

## SERP Feature Targeting

### Featured Snippets
- **Format**: paragraph (40-60 words), list, table
- **Placement**: after first paragraph or dedicated section
- **Answer directly**: question + immediate answer

### People Also Ask (PAA)
- Collect all PAA questions for target keyword
- Answer each in 2-3 sentences
- Use as H2/H3 headings in article

### Image Pack
- Optimize images with descriptive alt text
- Use descriptive filenames
- Compress for fast loading

## Competitor Analysis

### What to Analyze
1. Top 10 SERP results for target keyword
2. Content structure (headings, word count, images)
3. Topics covered vs missing
4. Backlink profile
5. Content freshness

### Gap Analysis Template
```
Competitor: [domain]
Content Length: [word count]
Structure: [heading count]
Topics Covered: [list]
Topics Missing: [what they don't cover]
Our Angle: [unique differentiator]
```

## Pitfalls
1. **90% of pages get no traffic** — validate search demand first
2. Don't target keywords without business potential
3. Traffic Potential > Search Volume
4. Intent mismatch = high bounce rate
5. Don't keyword stuff — Google's language matching is sophisticated

## Verification
- Keywords validated for search demand
- Intent classified correctly
- Competitor gaps identified
- Content brief includes SEO data
