# Link Outreach (Research-Based)

## Research Sources
- **Ahrefs**: Link building strategies ranked by effectiveness
- **Backlinko**: Skyscraper technique
- **Google**: Official link guidelines

## Link Building Methods (Ranked by Effectiveness)

### Method 1: Content-Driven Links (Best ROI)
**Create link-worthy content**: studies, tools, data, guides
**Why**: Natural links, scalable, no outreach needed
**Examples**: Original research, free tools, comprehensive guides
**Timeline**: 3-6 months for results

### Method 2: Guest Posting
**Write for other blogs** in your niche
**Why**: Controlled anchor text, relationship building
**Process**:
1. Find sites accepting guest posts
2. Pitch relevant topics
3. Write high-quality content
4. Include contextual link to your site

### Method 3: Broken Link Building
**Find broken links** → offer your replacement
**Why**: Win-win, high success rate
**Process**:
1. Find resource pages in your niche
2. Check for broken links (Ahrefs, Check My Links)
3. Create replacement content
4. Outreach with replacement suggestion

### Method 4: HARO/Connectively
**Respond to journalist queries**
**Why**: High-authority links (news sites, publications)
**Process**:
1. Sign up for HARO/Connectively
2. Monitor relevant queries
3. Respond with expertise
4. Get quoted with link

### Method 5: Skyscraper Technique (Backlinko)
**Find popular content** → **create better version** → **outreach**
**Why**: Proven content demand, clear targets
**Process**:
1. Find content with many backlinks
2. Create significantly better version (longer, newer, better)
3. Reach out to sites linking to original
4. Suggest your improved version

## Outreach Templates

### Guest Post Pitch
```
Subject: Guest Post Idea: [Topic]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Name] from {{DOMAIN}}. I read your article on [specific article] — great insights on [specific point].

I'd love to contribute a guest post on [proposed topic]:
1. [Point 1]
2. [Point 2]
3. [Point 3]

The article would be [word count] words, original, and not published elsewhere.

Would this be a good fit for [site]?

Best,
[Name]
```

### Broken Link Outreach
```
Subject: Broken Link on [Page Title]

Hi [Name],

I was reading your article on [topic] and found a broken link:
[broken URL]

I have a resource on [topic] that could work as a replacement:
[our URL]

Would you consider updating the link?

Thanks,
[Name]
```

## Link Quality Metrics

### Domain Rating (DR): 0-100
- **DR 0-20**: Low authority, easy to get
- **DR 20-50**: Medium authority, good targets
- **DR 50-80**: High authority, competitive
- **DR 80+**: Very high authority, very competitive

### What Makes a Good Link
- **Relevant**: topical match to your niche
- **In-content**: body text > footer/sidebar
- **Dofollow**: passes link equity
- **Traffic**: site gets real organic traffic
- **Natural anchor**: varied, not over-optimized

## Pitfalls
1. **Don't buy links** — Google penalty (manual action)
2. **Don't spam** — email reputation damage
3. **Don't over-optimize anchor text** — keep varied (brand, URL, generic)
4. **Track metrics**: outreach sent → responses → links acquired
5. **Quality > quantity**: 5 good links > 50 spam links
6. **Patience**: link building takes 3-6 months for results

## Verification
- Prospects qualified by DR and relevance
- Outreach emails personalized
- Response rate > 10%
- Links acquired logged and monitored
- No paid links or link schemes
